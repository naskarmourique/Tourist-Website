Heritage Sites in Kolkata

Victoria Memorial

A majestic marble building dedicated to Queen Victoria, this iconic monument is a blend of British and Mughal architecture and houses a museum showcasing the city's history.

Howrah Bridge

One of the busiest bridges in the world, the Howrah Bridge is an engineering marvel and a symbol of Kolkata. It connects the city to its twin city, Howrah, over the Hooghly River.

Indian Museum

Established in 1814, the Indian Museum is one of the oldest and largest museums in India, showcasing an extensive collection of artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and fossils.

Writer's Building

This historic building served as the office for writers of the British East India Company and later housed the West Bengal government's administrative offices.

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Situated on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Kali and is known for its beautiful architecture and serene surroundings.

Jorasanko Thakur Bari (Tagore House)

The ancestral home of the Tagore family, including Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore, is now a museum showcasing their life and works.

St. Paul's Cathedral

A fine example of Indo-Gothic architecture, this Anglican cathedral is renowned for its striking design and beautiful stained glass windows.

Marble Palace

Built in the 19th century by a wealthy Bengali merchant, this palatial mansion is known for its marble walls, European furniture, and impressive art collection.

Calcutta High Court

Established in 1862, the Calcutta High Court is one of the oldest high courts in India and is housed in a magnificent building featuring classical architecture.

Shaheed Minar (Ochterlony Monument)

Originally built to commemorate a British general, this monument was later rededicated to Indian freedom fighters and is an iconic landmark in Kolkata.
